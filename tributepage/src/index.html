Timeline of Sir Attenbouroughs life

  • 1926 Born in Isleworth, Middlesex, England. Attenborough spent his childhood collecting fossils, stones, and natural specimens
  • 1936 Attenborough and his brother Richard attended a lecture by Grey Owl (Archibald Belaney) at De Montfort Hall, Leicester, and were influenced by his advocacy of conservation.
  • 1950 applied for a job as a radio talk producer with the BBC unfortunatly was rejected
  • 1952 He joined the BBC full-time
  • 1965 Attenborough became the controller of BBC Two
  • 1969 he made a three-part series on the cultural history of the Indonesian island of Bali
  • 1979 Attenborough set about creating a body of work which became a benchmark of quality in wildlife film-making and influenced a generation of documentary film-makers.
  • 1990 The Trials of Life completed the original Life trilogy, looking at animal behaviour through the different stages of life.
  • 2013 Attenborough had collected 32 honorary degrees from British universities
  • 2016 A new British polar research ship was be named RRS Sir David Attenborough in his honour.
